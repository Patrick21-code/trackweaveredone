Our Story

Music has always
been about connection

TrackWeave was born from a simple question: what if you could see the invisible threads linking every song an artist has ever made? We built the tools to answer it.

Our Mission

Why we built TrackWeave

We believe music is richer when you understand the conversation happening across an artist's life's work.

Streaming platforms excel at delivering music on demand — but they're fundamentally designed around the moment of listening, not the act of understanding. You press play, a song ends, another begins. The deeper relational structure of a discography — the thematic evolution, the recurring motifs, the invisible threads connecting songs across decades — remains completely hidden.

"An artist's body of work isn't a collection of isolated tracks — it's a single, evolving conversation with themselves across time."

TrackWeave was built to make that conversation legible. By applying graph-based NLP analysis and SBERT embeddings to lyrics, metadata, and audio features, we construct a relational map of every artist's catalogue — revealing how themes migrate between albums, how early obsessions resurface years later, and how songs that seem unrelated are, in fact, deeply interconnected.

We pair this analytical layer with a social platform built for listeners who want to go deeper: log every album, contribute annotations to the shared knowledge graph, follow people whose taste you trust, and discover patterns that streaming algorithms were never designed to surface.

TrackWeave doesn't replace how you listen. It transforms what you hear.

The Technology

From lyrics to graph

Our analysis pipeline transforms raw discography data into interactive, queryable knowledge graphs — processing lyrics through semantic extraction, constructing weighted edges based on thematic similarity, and rendering force-directed visualizations in four distinct stages.

01

Ingestion

Lyrics, release metadata, audio features, and artist credits are collected and normalized across sources into a unified schema per track.

Data Layer
02

Semantic Extraction

Lyrics are processed using SBERT (Sentence-BERT) embeddings to generate semantic vector representations. Named entities, thematic motifs, emotional valence, and narrative patterns are identified, scored, and indexed for similarity computation.

Analysis Core
03

Graph Construction

Songs become nodes; weighted edges connect tracks based on cosine similarity scores between their SBERT embeddings. Edges above a configurable threshold form the network. Albums become colour-coded subgraphs, revealing both intra-album cohesion and cross-album thematic evolution.

Graph Engine
04

Visualisation

The graph is rendered using D3.js force-directed simulation with collision detection and dynamic node sizing. Users can zoom, pan, filter by album, adjust similarity thresholds, search for specific songs, and add community annotations to any node or edge.

Every node tells a story,
every edge reveals a connection

In TrackWeave's graph model, songs are nodes and thematic relationships are edges. Edge weights reflect computed similarity scores derived from SBERT embeddings — capturing shared lyrical themes, emotional tone, narrative structure, and semantic patterns between tracks.

Albums form natural colour-coded clusters within the force-directed layout, while cross-album edges expose thematic evolution and recurring motifs across an artist's career. The central hub nodes represent songs with the highest connectivity — thematic anchors that bridge multiple albums and narrative threads.

What We Believe

The principles behind
every decision we make

1

Depth over virality

We are not optimising for clicks, shares, or engagement streaks. We are building a space for listeners who want to slow down and truly understand the music they love. Every feature we ship is measured against this.

2

The listener is the expert

Our algorithms surface patterns — but human ears and human memory hold meanings that no model can fully capture. Community annotations are treated as first-class data, not decorations on top of automated output.

3

Transparency in analysis

When we say two songs are connected, we show you why — which themes overlapped, what the edge weight means, and what the underlying model found. We never reduce music to an opaque score.

4

Privacy by design

Your listening history, reviews, and ratings belong to you. We do not sell your data, we do not profile you for third-party advertising, and we provide full data export at any time, no questions asked.

Milestones

How we got here

September 2023

The idea

Alex and Soo-Yeon sketch the first version of the graph model on a whiteboard at a hackathon. They win third place. They disagree with the judges to this day.

January 2024

First prototype

A working analysis pipeline processes the complete discography of three artists and produces its first graph. Edge weights are rough, but the patterns are immediately visible and surprising.

April 2024

Team grows

Marcus, Lena, Jonas, and Clara join. The product goes from a research demo to a working platform with a design language, a social layer, and a proper backend.

October 2024

Private beta

Five hundred listeners join the private beta. They log over 12,000 albums in the first month and write annotations that meaningfully improve the graph model's accuracy.

March 2025

Public launch

TrackWeave opens to everyone. The catalogue grows to over 180,000 artists and 2.4 million songs. The graph now contains more than 9 million edges.

Now

Still building

We are actively expanding the analysis model, improving the visualisation engine, and working closely with our community to make the graph smarter with every annotation.
