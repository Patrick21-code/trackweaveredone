Our Story

Music has always
been about connection

TrackWeave was born from a simple question: what if you could see the invisible threads linking every song an artist has ever made? We built the tools to answer it.

Our Mission

Why we built TrackWeave

We believe music is richer when you understand the conversation happening across an artist's life's work.

Streaming platforms are extraordinary at delivering music on demand — but they are fundamentally designed around the moment of listening, not the act of understanding. You press play, a song ends, another begins. The deeper architecture of a discography remains invisible.

"An artist's body of work is a single, evolving argument — a conversation with themselves across time."

TrackWeave was created to make that conversation legible. By applying graph-based natural language processing to lyrics, metadata, and audio features, we construct a relational map of every artist's catalogue — revealing how themes migrate between albums, how early obsessions resurface years later, and how songs that seem unrelated are, in fact, deeply connected.

We pair this analytical layer with a social platform built for listeners who want to go further: log every album, write honest reviews, follow people whose ears you trust, and contribute annotations to the shared graph of human musical understanding.

TrackWeave is not a replacement for how you listen. It is a lens that changes what you hear.

The Technology

From lyrics to graph

Our NLP pipeline processes a discography end-to-end — from raw text to an interactive, queryable knowledge graph — in four stages.

01

Ingestion

Lyrics, release metadata, audio features, and artist credits are collected and normalized across sources into a unified schema per track.

Data Layer
02

Semantic Extraction

Each track's lyrics are tokenized and embedded using transformer-based models. Named entities, motifs, emotional valence, and thematic clusters are identified and scored.

NLP Core
03

Graph Construction

Songs become nodes; weighted edges form between tracks sharing themes, sonic fingerprints, narrative arcs, or lyrical references. Albums become colour-coded subgraphs.

Graph Engine
04

Visualisation

The graph is rendered as an interactive canvas. Users can zoom, filter by album, inspect edge weights, and add community annotations to any node or connection.

Graph Anatomy Hover nodes to inspect

Every node tells a story,
every edge reveals one

In TrackWeave's graph model, songs are nodes and relationships are edges. The weight of each edge reflects the computed similarity between two tracks — derived from shared lyrical themes, emotional tone, sonic characteristics, and narrative structure.

Albums form natural colour-coded clusters, while cross-album edges expose the thematic evolution of an artist across their career. The artist node sits apart — a root from which all creative work extends.

What We Believe

The principles behind
every decision we make

1

Depth over virality

We are not optimising for clicks, shares, or engagement streaks. We are building a space for listeners who want to slow down and truly understand the music they love. Every feature we ship is measured against this.

2

The listener is the expert

Our algorithms surface patterns — but human ears and human memory hold meanings that no model can fully capture. Community annotations are treated as first-class data, not decorations on top of automated output.

3

Transparency in analysis

When we say two songs are connected, we show you why — which themes overlapped, what the edge weight means, and what the underlying model found. We never reduce music to an opaque score.

4

Privacy by design

Your listening history, reviews, and ratings belong to you. We do not sell your data, we do not profile you for third-party advertising, and we provide full data export at any time, no questions asked.

Milestones

How we got here

September 2023

The idea

Alex and Soo-Yeon sketch the first version of the graph model on a whiteboard at a hackathon. They win third place. They disagree with the judges to this day.

January 2024

First prototype

A working NLP pipeline processes the complete discography of three artists and produces its first graph. Edge weights are rough, but the patterns are immediately visible and surprising.

April 2024

Team grows

Marcus, Lena, Jonas, and Clara join. The product goes from a research demo to a product with a design language, a social layer, and a proper backend.

October 2024

Private beta

Five hundred listeners join the private beta. They log over 12,000 albums in the first month and write annotations that meaningfully improve the graph model's accuracy.

March 2025

Public launch

TrackWeave opens to everyone. The catalogue grows to over 180,000 artists and 2.4 million songs. The graph now contains more than 9 million edges.

Now

Still building

We are actively expanding the NLP model, improving the visualisation engine, and working closely with our community to make the graph smarter with every annotation.
